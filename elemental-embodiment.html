5-Session Series

A structured path into the body.

An intentional 5-session series designed to offer a structured space for you to explore the relationship between your breath and your nervous system. Rather than a one-off reset, this series moves through a progressive framework—building familiarity with somatic tools that support your body's capacity for regulation.

Why a series instead of a single session?

Single breathwork sessions can be profound, but they often function more like a deep breath than a real shift in capacity. If your nervous system has been holding patterns for years—decades—a single session is unlikely to rewire that.

A five-session series gives us the runway to build progressively: from mechanics, to capacity, to deeper somatic exploration, to integration. By the end, you have a relationship with your breath you didn't have when you started.

The arc of the series

We begin with the mechanics of the breath itself and your CO2 tolerance. This is the foundation. The goal is to build a stronger container—a nervous system that feels more resilient in the face of daily stressors.

From there, we move into somatic exploration. This may include inner-child prompts, forgiveness practices, or other somatic frames that allow you to acknowledge and move through stagnant emotional energy. The breath is the vehicle. The body does the work.

Each session is designed to help you drop out of the thinking mind and into a more embodied state—providing a dedicated space for reflection and quiet that's hard to come by in modern life.

Who this is for

You're interested in a structured, step-by-step approach to breathwork rather than a one-off experience. You want to explore how somatic tools like inner-child work and forgiveness can support your overall wellness. You're navigating the tired-but-wired state that often accompanies chronic inflammation, and you're looking for a dedicated practice to help.

What's included

  • 5 one-on-one virtual sessions
  • Progressive somatic framework
  • Inner-child & forgiveness practices
  • Recorded practices for between sessions
  • Integration support throughout
